Immigr8: Improving the journey of Migrant workers

  • Business design
 back

Summary

In our globalized world there is increased economic migration to high income countries like Canada. Economic migrants face the problem of finding relevant information without wasting precious time. The concept of a crowdsourced AI platform was proposed as a solution - by providing personalized information based on the userâ€™s unique profile. A multi-sided business model was designed with big picture numbers showing profitability in 3 years.

Challenge

To explore and identity opportunities for innovation along the Migrant worker’s immigration journey, to ideate and evaluate solutions and to create a strategy for the implementation of the tested strategy.

Research

Users: The following user personas and customer journey were developed after analysing the data acquired from in-depth interviews exploring the journeys of users within the target market and supported by secondary data sources.

Insights:

  • Users spend a lot of time and effort finding information from different sources and putting it together like a puzzle
  • While all the steps of the process are the same, every immigrant’s journey is different in the details
  • Users constantly seeks out support and reassurance because they want to reduce the risks and improve their chances of success

Define: Reframing the problem

How might we provide personalized information and support from one source to our users as they go through the Express Entry immigration journey in a time saving and affordable way?

Concept: Immigration AI Bot

Using data gathered from users who have gone through the Express Entry process to Canada, we provide personalized information to users via an intelligent virtual assistant that lives on your phone. I named the solution “Immigr8”.

How it works (Video)

Deliver: Prototyping and testing

The key assumptions regarding the problem / solution fit was tested using in depth exploratory interviews.

  • Users were surprised when they calculated the average amount of time they had spent just gathering information and were interested in reducing that time
  • Users confirmed that they struggled with finding information
  • Users confirmed that the solution concept which curated information on the process would be valuable
