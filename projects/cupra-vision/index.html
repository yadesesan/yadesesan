project

Cupra Vision: A vision of sports driving

  • Human-Centred Design
 back

Summary

CUPRA is an offshoot of the spanish automobile manufacturer SEAT. As a relatively new automobile brand, a major objective is to find ways to stand out in the market by finding a niche for themselves. Targeting sport driving aficionados means creating experiences that they are able to connect with. In this project, we created a concept of sports driving using digital and connectivity technology called Cupra Vision.

Challenge

What does the vision of an individualised CUPRA sports driving experience look like?

Research

We started our process by trying to understand the environment in which CUPRA does business in. We explored the automobile industry, the company, their competitors and partners as well as the general context using desk based research to lay a foundation and 13 in-depth interviews with users.

Insights

We synthesized the information gathered during the research phase to create an image of who the target user is as well as key insights that form the basis of identifying opportunities for applying design intervention.

Problem Reframing

How might we put more control in Carlos’ hands when he’s sports driving in uncertain conditions?

Design principles

  • Non-intrusive: One of the things that is important to our user is not being distracted by technology when he is “in the zone”.
  • Use existing hardware: This client imposed constraint limits the scope of our concepts to those that do not require radical changes to the hardware already available in current models

Ideation

We ideated some possible solutions for our persona, Carlos, and used the impact - value matrix to help us decide which ideas to prototype and test with users

Concept: Cupra Vision

We designed an enhanced navigation interface that lives in the driver’s line of sight for performance driving in sports mode.

Prototyping and Testing

We used various techniqes of different fidelities to test our concepts and gain further insight into our final product.
