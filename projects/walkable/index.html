Walkable: Designing a walking experience

  • interaction design
 back

Challenge

Design a way to turn a passive commute into an active interaction? How do we provide the walking commuter with opportunities for exploration and adventure?

My Role

Researcher / Ideation / Interaction Design

Research and Insights

The team I was working with carried out in-depth interviews with walking commuters in the Gracia area of Barcelona. The following are some insights that we uncovered.

  • Commuters rarely change their route to work or school for years
  • Only 1 in 5 people have consciously explored their neighbourhoods
  • A walking commuterâ€™s mood changes depending on whether she is going to or coming back from work

Solution

We generated some ideas but finally landed on the concept for an app that shows the user events, discounts, new spots, photospots and street art in their neighbourhood. We called it Walkable.

User Storyboards

User Flows and Wireframes

The storyboard helped us to design a user flow showing the actions and choices available to the user. We then created low fidelity wireframes and used them to generate more ideas and test the value of the solution.
